Consent Agreement

Welcome to Columbia University's online tobacco cessation education website. This educational website was produced through collaborative efforts of Dr David Albert from the College of Dental Medicine and the Columbia Center for New Media Teaching and Learning (CCCNMTL) to provide an easy to use online instructional tool for post-doctoral dental residents.

These multimedia tobacco cessation counseling modules include information on tobacco cessation pharmacotherapies, demonstrations videos, treatment activities and immediate response quizzes. Each teaching module is tailored to a different dental specialty such as oral surgery, periodontics, prosthodontics, and general practice.

It is our intent that learners come away with a strong understanding of available tobacco cessation pharmacotherapies and the empowerment to effectively provide tobacco cessation information to patients in their future dental practices.

We also invite you to participate in a research study that consists of two surveys to evaluate the course. Participation in the research study is voluntary. You are not required to participate in the research study. If you agree to participate we will provide you with a pre-course survey and a post-course survey. There will be no compensation for participation in this study.

Data analyzed in this study will be anonymized. There are no risks associated with participation. The information gained from this study will help improve tobacco education for pre-doctoral dental students and postdoctoral dental residents. The Columbia University Medical Center IRB, the Office for Human Research Protections and members of the research team may have access to study data.

By completing the registration page you are agreeing to participate in this research study.

If there are any questions or concerns please contact the Principal Investigator, Dr. David Albert at the Columbia University College of Dental Medicine at 212- 342-8588 or by email at daa1@columbia.edu.

You may also contact the Columbia University Medical Center Institutional Review Board (IRB) at 212-305-5883 (reference # IRB- AAAL4455).
